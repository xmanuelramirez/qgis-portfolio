01 Hydraulic Sectorization

Water Tank Pressure Zone Delineation

📍 Ciudad Juárez, Chihuahua

+
QGIS DTM / Raster Analysis PyQGIS AutoCAD Integration Pressure Analysis · 10 m.c.a.

Determined the hydraulic service reach of each potable water storage tank using gravitational pressure analysis. Tank locations were extracted from AutoCAD drawings and combined with the city's Digital Terrain Model to compute raster-based influence zones — identifying the exact coverage area each tank can serve at up to 10 m.c.a. (meters of water column) of residual pressure. The output directly informed the city's water network sectorization and pressure district boundaries.

  • ◆ Computed gravitational pressure head per tank from DTM elevation differentials to determine hydraulic service reach
  • ◆ Generated raster-based influence zones clipped at the 10 m.c.a. pressure threshold per storage tank
  • ◆ Produced municipal-scale sectorization cartography to define pressure district boundaries for network planning

03 Hydraulic Capacity & Risk

Urban Stream Hydraulic Assessment

📍 León, Guanajuato

+
QGIS 0.5 m LiDAR DTM Hydraulic Analysis Field Survey 25-yr Design Storm Traffic-light Classification

Assessed the hydraulic capacity and risk status of urban streams across León's watershed system. Using a 0.5 m LiDAR DTM combined with field surveys, longitudinal and cross-sectional profiles were extracted for each arroyo. Design storm runoff (25-year return period) was computed per contributing catchment and compared against channel capacity, maximum erosive velocity thresholds, and minimum sedimentation flow limits. Each stream reach received a traffic-light risk classification to guide urban drainage and retention decisions.

  • ◆ Extracted longitudinal and cross-sectional profiles from 0.5 m DTM validated with field measurements; computed hydraulic capacity in m³/s per reach
  • ◆ Calculated 25-year design storm runoff per watershed; identified maximum erosive and minimum sedimentation velocity thresholds per channel segment
  • ◆ Delivered traffic-light risk classification per reach:   ● Saturated ● Conditional ● Available
